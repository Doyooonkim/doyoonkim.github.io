About

I study how people make prosocial decisions when cognitive frameworks, not just incentives, are the binding constraint. My research introduces the regime-selection framework, which endogenizes the adoption of evaluative frames in individual decision-making, providing microfoundations for behavioral interventions that target competence rather than compliance.

My broader research program extends this foundation to collective welfare analysis and game-theoretic models of communicative coordination. I am also interested in applied domains where the regime-selection framework may prove fruitful, including creativity, innovation, and human–AI complementarity.
